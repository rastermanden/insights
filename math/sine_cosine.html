The unit circle has radius 1. For any angle θ, place a point on the circle at that angle from the positive x-axis. The point's coordinates are (cos θ, sin θ).

Cosine is the horizontal distance — how far left or right the point is. Sine is the vertical distance — how far up or down. Both oscillate smoothly between −1 and 1 as θ sweeps around the circle. Tangent is sin θ / cos θ, the slope of the line from the origin to the point.
